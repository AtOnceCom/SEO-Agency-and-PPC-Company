## PPC strategy for B2B technology companies

A good B2B technology PPC strategy can start with clarity. That includes: what counts as a lead, what makes a lead qualified, and what a buyer searches before talking to sales.

AtOnce can help map PPC to the buying journey.

That can involve:

1. **Discovery keywords:** Searches that show a problem or job-to-be-done
2. **Comparison keywords:** Searches that involve alternatives, competitors, and reviews
3. **Product keywords:** Searches that include the product category and key features
4. **Brand keywords:** Searches for brand and product names, plus close variants

Not every account uses all of these in the same way. For some B2B SaaS brands, competitor terms can be expensive or lower intent. For other brands, those terms can be a strong lead source.

AtOnce can test and adjust based on performance data.

## Keyword and audience research that can fit B2B tech

B2B tech PPC keyword research can be more than collecting a list of terms. It can also include intent sorting, match type planning, and negative keyword ideas.

AtOnce can research:

- **High-intent keywords:** Terms that can signal buying interest
- **Feature keywords:** Terms tied to product capabilities and integrations
- **Industry keywords:** Terms tied to vertical markets, like finance or healthcare
- **Use case keywords:** Terms tied to outcomes, like automation or reporting
- **Exclusion keywords:** Searches that can bring the wrong clicks

Audience targeting can also support PPC performance. Depending on the account, that can involve remarketing and customer match lists, when available.
