## Core parts of SaaS PPC and Google Ads that AtOnce can handle

SaaS Google Ads can involve search campaigns, brand campaigns, competitor terms, retargeting, and landing pages for each offer. Not every account needs every piece, but each piece can matter.

### Search campaigns for high-intent SaaS buyers

Search ads can work well when people already want a solution. That means keyword choice matters a lot.

Work can include:

- **High-intent keyword lists** based on product category and use case  
- **Tighter ad groups** so ads match searches more closely  
- **Negative keyword plans** to limit poor-fit traffic  
- **Ad extensions** that can add more useful details  

For SaaS, search intent can vary by stage. Some people search for “best software for…” while others search for a named product. AtOnce can map that intent into a campaign structure that can stay organized.

### Brand and non-brand campaign structure

Many SaaS accounts mix brand and non-brand traffic, which can make reporting confusing. Separating campaigns can make performance easier to read.

AtOnce can help set up structure that can involve:

- **Brand campaigns** for people searching the product name  
- **Non-brand campaigns** for category and problem searches  
- **Competitor campaigns** where allowed and appropriate  
- **Separate budgets** so brand traffic does not hide non-brand issues  

This structure can help teams see what is really driving growth.

### Ad copy that can match SaaS buyers and product value

Ad copy can affect click-through rate and lead quality. SaaS buyers can respond better when the ad is clear and specific, not vague.

AtOnce can help with ad copy that can include:

Copy can also match brand voice. That matters when a SaaS brand has a strong tone and style.
