## Where industrial Google Ads accounts can break down

A lot of wasted effort in Google Ads comes from unclear structure and unclear measurement. When tracking is incomplete, it gets hard to decide what to scale and what to pause.

Issues industrial accounts can run into include:

- **Broad targeting** that pulls in students, job seekers, or DIY traffic  
- **Keywords that mix intent** such as research searches grouped with purchase-ready searches  
- **Ad groups that are too large** so ad copy can’t match the search well  
- **Landing pages that don’t match** the query, product, or service area  
- **Conversions that are not defined** so reporting shows clicks, but not quality  
- **No consistent testing** for ads and landing page changes  

AtOnce can audit the account, then prioritize changes that can be made safely without guessing.

## A clear process can help reduce wasted spend

Industrial ad accounts can grow over time, and changes add up. A process can keep the account understandable and help reduce wasted spend.

An example process can involve:

1. **Audit and measurement review**  
   Conversion actions, account structure, search terms, and campaign settings can be reviewed to find gaps.

2. **Targeting and keyword plan**  
   Keyword groups can be organized by product line, service type, or intent level. Negative keyword themes can be added early.

3. **Ad copy and extensions**  
   Ads can be rewritten to match real buying terms. Extensions can be used to add trust details like categories, services, or contact options.

4. **Landing page alignment**  
   Paid traffic can be sent to pages that match the query and reduce confusion. Forms and calls-to-action can be improved for clarity.

5. **Ongoing optimization and reporting**  
   Weekly adjustments can be made based on what the data shows, including search terms, conversion rates, and lead quality signals.

This process can change depending on the account. AtOnce can adapt the order based on what creates the clearest lift first.
